# Double Blaze: Tier Messaging

Voice: Double Blaze. Calm, plainspoken, confident. National-brand depth applied to local problems, by neighbors invested in your success. Concrete over clever. No em dashes anywhere.

The spine: Sell, Run, Grow, Scale. Each tier is tied to the customer's order-to-cash, and each one adds something the tier below it does not. This is the ladder logic that keeps the tiers distinct.

The current site undersells all four. The fix is to stop describing a website and start describing what the business gets.

---

## The through-line (use above the tiers)

Most of what slows a business down lives between the first click and the money in the door. We build that whole path, the selling, the running, the growing, and we tie all of it to one thing: your order to cash. National-brand depth, at Main Street prices, by people who live here too.

---

## Green Trail, $199 a month. Sell.

**Position:** This is not a website. It is the complete, customer-facing way your business takes orders, books work, and gets paid. Everything from the first click to money in the door.

**What it looks like in your business:**
- Restaurants: online ordering, delivery tracking, curbside "I'm here" arrival, and daily promotions. The experience people already expect from Chick-fil-A or Shake Shack, running on your own site.
- Retail, apparel, auto sales: full purchase and checkout on your site, with ship, local delivery, in-store pickup, and returns. A real store, not a brochure.
- Agents, clinics, contractors, real estate, insurance: appointment setting and scheduling, client check-in, monthly billing, invoicing, and the customer records that keep it all straight.

**The line that stops the undersell:** The full order-to-cash front end of your business, live and working, for $199 a month.

---

## Blue Trail, $499 a month. Run.

**Position:** Green Trail brings the money in. Blue Trail runs everything behind it. The administrative work that eats your nights and weekends, automated and built around how your business actually operates.

**What it looks like in your business:**
- Restaurants: accounting tied to your sales, inventory that tracks itself, and shift and labor scheduling.
- Retail, auto: service and maintenance reminders that bring customers back, employee scheduling, and books that reconcile against your real orders.
- Agents, clinics, contractors: the follow-ups, paperwork, and admin you dread, customized to your workflow and automated so it simply happens.

**The line that stops the undersell:** Every dollar that comes through Green Trail is tracked, reconciled, and automated in Blue Trail. The parts of the job you hate, handled, tailored to you.

---

## Black Trail, $999 a month. Grow.

**Position:** Once selling and running are handled, Black Trail goes on offense. Campaigns, content, and social presence, all pointed at more customers and measured where you can see it.

**What it looks like in your business:**
- Marketing campaigns built and run for you, not just advised on.
- Content and social media managed on-brand and consistently, without you touching it.
- One owner's dashboard, current and meaningful, that you can open every morning and actually understand: what is working, what it is producing, and what to do next.

**The line that stops the undersell:** Selling and running keep the business alive. Black Trail makes it bigger, and puts the proof in front of you every day.

---

## Double Black, $1,499 a month. Scale.

**Position:** For a business ready to operate like a much larger one. We connect your tools, automate across departments, and put real intelligence to work, the kind of capability that usually costs ten times this.

**What it looks like in your business:**
- Your third-party apps integrated so everything talks to everything, with one source of truth.
- Department-level workflows for larger teams, or advanced systems for smaller businesses that work better than what the giants run.
- Predictive modeling: what is coming, what to stock, who to call, and where the next dollar is.

**The line that stops the undersell:** Advanced systems for a smaller business that outperform the advanced systems at the largest ones. That is the whole point.

## Applying this to the Services page

The current Services page leads with capability categories (Websites, Apps, Social, Consulting) described in soft terms. Reframe it to lead with the Sell, Run, Grow, Scale ladder and the order-to-cash story, so a visitor sees the depth before the price. The capability cards can stay as a secondary "how we build it" section, but the headline promise should be what the business gets, not what we make.

Suggested card rewrites if you keep the four-card layout:
- Websites that work becomes: Sell on day one. A storefront that takes orders, books appointments, and gets you paid, not a brochure.
- Apps, built to ship becomes: Real products, not slideware. Mobile and web apps from a team that ships, tied into how you actually run.
- Social and digital marketing becomes: Grow on purpose. Campaigns and content run for you, measured on a dashboard you check daily.
- Technology consulting becomes: Process and data first. We get the foundation right before we automate, so the technology actually pays off.

---

## Two things to keep in lockstep

1. Differentiation. The Sell, Run, Grow, Scale spine is what keeps a powerful $199 tier from making the higher tiers look optional. Keep that ladder visible everywhere the tiers appear, so the reason to move up is obvious.
2. Marketing and delivery alignment. This copy promises a lot at $199. Make sure the standardized Green Trail build actually ships the order-to-cash front end described here, because under Trail Run the customer gets it free for 30 days and judges us on results. The promise and the build need to match.

Note on Trail Run: during the free window everyone runs on Blue Trail, so the Trail Run experience is Green's order-to-cash front end plus Blue's back-office automation, both at once. That makes the tier reframe and the Trail Run offer reinforce each other.
