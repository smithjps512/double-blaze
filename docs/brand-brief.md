# Brand Brief

Drop this in the repo (for example `docs/brand-brief.md`) so all pages share one voice and identity. Color tokens here match the spec; pages beyond the homepage should draw voice and framing from this file.

## Positioning
Double Blaze Solutions is a veteran-owned technology company serving the New River Valley, Roanoke Valley, and Martinsville/Danville, giving local businesses the caliber of technology and product expertise usually reserved for national brands, delivered by neighbors invested in the region's success.

## Mission and promise
We build technology that strengthens our community: practical solutions for local businesses, and products that make life in the region a little better. We bring big-league experience home and put it to work for the people next door.

## Brand story
On the Appalachian Trail a single blaze keeps you on the path, and a double blaze signals a turn ahead and tells you to pay attention. That is our name. Technology is full of those turns for a growing business, and we help you read the trail and take the right next step. The company is led by a founder with two decades building digital products for the world's biggest brands in sports and entertainment, and by a Coast Guard veteran whose service shapes how we work: show up, follow through, take care of people.

## Value proposition
National-brand technology expertise, delivered locally, by a team that wins only when the community wins.

## Audiences
1. Primary (revenue): local small and midsize businesses needing websites, apps, social media, and technology consulting.
2. Secondary (users): residents and students who use our products.
3. Future: regional institutions and public-sector buyers served by the certified veteran-owned entity.

## Message pillars
1. Rooted here.
2. Enterprise-grade, locally delivered.
3. Veteran-owned and service-driven.
4. Technology that serves people.

## Voice
Warm, grounded, confident, plainspoken. A capable neighbor, not a coastal agency. Concrete over buzzwords. No jargon, no hype, no em dashes.

## Taglines
- Primary: `Enterprise technology. New River Valley roots.`
- Supporting: `Solutions that strengthen our community.`
- Alternates: `Big-league technology for local business.` / `Helping local business read the trail ahead.`

## Boilerplate
Short: `Double Blaze Solutions is a veteran-owned technology company serving the New River Valley, Roanoke Valley, and Martinsville/Danville, building websites, apps, and digital solutions that help local businesses grow.`

## Track record (team and founder experience)
Frame as the team's career experience, not Double Blaze company projects. Subject to NDA confirmation, the team has built for Disney, Universal Studios, Procter & Gamble, Sony, Diageo, WPP, the NFL, NASCAR, and more, including software products (iGoHD, Fan View, Node Explorer, Game View, Melissa for Educators, Domain-U/Lumineo, VocalPoint), themed guest experiences, ad-network and mobile campaign technology, and operations automation. Use selectively on the About and Work pages; keep the homepage proof bar short.

Accessibility: use Impact Orange (or darker) for orange text and links; reserve Trail Orange for buttons and large accents. Target WCAG 2.2 AA.

## Typography direction
Confident, slightly condensed sans for headlines (for example Archivo or Space Grotesk), clean readable sans for body (for example Inter or Source Sans). A slab serif such as Zilla Slab is an option for warmer headlines.

## Logo direction
Two simple vertical bars like painted trail blazes, in maroon and orange, optionally offset to suggest forward motion. Works as a single-color icon at small sizes. Wordmark sets "DOUBLE BLAZE" prominently with "SOLUTIONS" smaller.

## Trademark caution
The maroon and orange draw on Virginia Tech's palette for local familiarity, but the shades are our own. Never use VT logos, marks, or anything implying university affiliation.
